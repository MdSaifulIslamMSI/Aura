# Dashboard Information Design

Design dashboards for fast scanning and confident decision-making. Put the signal first and reduce visual noise that competes with the data.

## Workflow

1. Identify the primary questions the dashboard must answer.
2. Group information into summary, trend, drill-down, and operational layers.
3. Place the most decision-relevant metrics in the strongest visual positions.
4. Keep filters and date controls visible without dominating the layout.
5. Use contrast, spacing, and typography to separate summary from detail.

## Good Defaults

- Lead with a concise KPI rail, not a crowded wall of cards.
- Give one or two charts the visual focus instead of making every panel equal.
- Keep tables readable and close to the filters that affect them.
- Use color sparingly so alerts and thresholds still stand out.

## Guardrails

- Avoid decorative gradients behind charts.
- Avoid repeating the same metric in too many formats.
- Avoid tiny text and over-dense card grids that make scanning harder.

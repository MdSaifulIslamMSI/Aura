# Style Directions

## Editorial Contrast

- Use when the product needs authority, taste, or a stronger brand signature.
- Type: condensed or high-contrast display paired with a practical UI sans.
- Color: disciplined neutrals with one vivid accent.
- Layout: asymmetry, strong crops, decisive whitespace.
- Avoid: tiny supporting text and weak hierarchy below the fold.

## Precision Commerce

- Use for marketplaces, fintech, dense product surfaces, and high-comparison screens.
- Type: practical grotesk with optional mono for prices or meta.
- Color: stable neutral base with one action color and restrained status tones.
- Surfaces: crisp cards, clear borders, readable density.
- Avoid: excessive blur, decorative shadows, or oversized motion.

## Premium Warmth

- Use for high-ticket retail, interiors, travel, or fashion.
- Type: refined serif display or high-contrast sans plus a calm UI sans.
- Color: parchment, deep neutrals, restrained accent tones.
- Surfaces: large imagery, low chrome, generous breathing room.
- Avoid: coupon-like badge noise and crowded promotional rails.

## Playful Utility

- Use for youth brands, creator tools, and learning products that need energy without chaos.
- Type: rounder grotesk, roomy spacing, bold labels.
- Color: saturated accents on dependable neutrals.
- Motion: springier but still purposeful.
- Avoid: turning every component into a sticker or badge.
